What is Cervical Cancer?

The Importance of Screening (Smear Tests)

Regular cervical screening (also known as a smear test) can detect abnormal cell changes in the cervix, allowing for early treatment and preventing cancer from developing. It's a quick and simple procedure that can save your life.

In the UK, women aged 25-49 are invited for screening every 3 years, and women aged 50-64 every 5 years. If you're eligible, please attend your appointment.

Symptoms to Be Aware Of

In the early stages, cervical cancer often has no symptoms. However, as it progresses, you may experience:

  • Unusual vaginal bleeding, such as bleeding between periods, after sex, or after menopause
  • Changes to your vaginal discharge
  • Pain during sex
  • Pelvic pain

If you experience any of these symptoms, it's important to see your GP as soon as possible.

Where to Find Support

If you've been diagnosed with cervical cancer, or are concerned about your risk, there are many organisations that can provide support and information:

  • Jo's Cervical Cancer Trust
  • Macmillan Cancer Support
